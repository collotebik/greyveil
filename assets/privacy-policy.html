1. Who We Are

Greyveil Technologies ("Greyveil", "we", "us", "our") is a cybersecurity and technology firm registered in Kenya, operating at Nairobi, Kenya. We are the Data Controller for personal data collected through our website and services.

Data Protection Contact: [email protected] or +254 741 733 851.

2. Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity data — first name, last name, job title, company name.
  • Contact data — email address, phone number, physical address.
  • Technical data — IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages visited, and time on site (collected via analytics tools).
  • Communication data — messages, enquiries, and correspondence you send us via our contact form, email, or WhatsApp.
  • Service data — information relating to the services we deliver, including system and network details provided in the course of a security engagement (governed separately by your service agreement).

We do not collect sensitive personal data (e.g. health, biometric, or financial data) through this website.

3. How We Collect Your Data

  • Directly from you — when you fill in our contact or enquiry form, email us, call us, or message us on WhatsApp.
  • Automatically — when you visit our website, we may collect technical data through cookies and analytics tools (see Section 10).
  • From third parties — referrals, professional networks such as LinkedIn, or publicly available sources, in accordance with applicable law.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide you with information about our services.
  • To deliver contracted cybersecurity, IT, and software services.
  • To send you service-related communications and updates (not marketing, unless you have opted in).
  • To improve and maintain our website and service quality.
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations under Kenyan law.
  • To protect our legitimate business interests and enforce our agreements.

We will never sell your personal data to third parties.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the KDPA, we process your data on the following lawful bases:

  • Consent — where you have provided explicit consent (e.g. opting into communications).
  • Contract performance — where processing is necessary to deliver services you have engaged us for.
  • Legitimate interests — where we have a legitimate business interest that does not override your rights (e.g. responding to enquiries, securing our systems).
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to process data to comply with applicable Kenyan law.

6. Sharing Your Data

We may share your personal data with:

  • Service providers — trusted third parties who assist us in operating our website and delivering services (e.g. form handling via Formspree, analytics). These parties process data only on our instructions.
  • Professional advisors — lawyers, accountants, and insurers, subject to confidentiality obligations.
  • Regulators and authorities — where required by Kenyan law, court order, or regulatory demand.

We do not transfer personal data outside Kenya unless adequate safeguards (as defined under the KDPA) are in place.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes it was collected, or as required by law. In general:

  • Enquiry and contact data — up to 2 years from last contact.
  • Client service data — for the duration of the engagement plus 5 years, in line with standard contractual limitation periods.
  • Technical/analytics data — up to 12 months.

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

8. Security

As a cybersecurity firm, we apply industry-leading technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These include encryption in transit, access controls, and regular security reviews of our internal systems.

However, no internet transmission is 100% secure. If you believe your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately at [email protected].

9. Your Rights Under the KDPA

As a data subject under Kenya's Data Protection Act 2019, you have the following rights:

  • Right to be informed — to know how your data is being used (this policy fulfils that obligation).
  • Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your data where there is no lawful reason to retain it.
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw at any time without affecting prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at [email protected]. We will respond within 21 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) of Kenya at odpc.go.ke.

10. Cookies

Our website uses a small number of cookies to improve your experience:

  • Essential cookies — required for the site to function (e.g. remembering your cookie consent preference). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies — used to understand how visitors use our site (e.g. Google Analytics, if enabled). These are only set with your consent.

You can control cookie preferences via your browser settings at any time. Disabling analytics cookies will not affect your use of the site.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Material changes that affect how we process your data will be communicated directly where we hold your contact details.
